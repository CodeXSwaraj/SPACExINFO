Galactic

Space is big. Really big. And it's full of weird and wonderful things. Let's take a closer look.

The Milky Way

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy, about 100,000 light-years across. It contains 200-400 billion stars, 100 billion planets, and 4 billion habitable planets. The Solar System is located within the disk, about 27,000 light-years away from the Galactic Center.

What are black holes?

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing can escape from it. The theory of general relativity predicts that a sufficiently compact mass can deform spacetime to form a black hole. The boundary of the region from which no escape is possible is called the event horizon.

Why is the sky blue?

Rayleigh scattering is the scattering of light by particles smaller than the wavelength being scattered. It causes the blue color of the sky during the day and the red-orange color of the sun at sunrise and sunset.

How big is the universe?

The observable Universe is about 93 billion light-years in diameter. The Universe is expanding and has been since the Big Bang occurred about 13.8 billion years ago.

How many planets are there?

There are 8 confirmed planets in the Solar System: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune. There are 5 dwarf planets: Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, and Ceres. There are also over 4,000 confirmed exoplanets orbiting other stars.

What is a supernova?

A supernova is a stellar explosion that briefly outshines an entire galaxy, radiating as much energy as the Sun is expected to emit over its entire life span. This occurs during the last evolutionary stages of a massive star or when a white dwarf is triggered into runaway nuclear fusion.

What is a comet?

A comet is an icy small Solar System body that, when passing close to the Sun, heats up and begins to outgas, displaying a visible atmosphere or coma, and sometimes also a tail. These phenomena are due to the effects of solar radiation and the solar wind upon the nucleus of the comet.

What is a quasar?

A quasar is an extremely luminous active galactic nucleus (AGN) that is powered by a supermassive black hole. It is believed that the intense brightness of quasars results from the accretion of material around the supermassive black hole.

What is dark matter?

Dark matter is a hypothetical form of matter that is thought to account for approximately 85% of the total matter in the Universe and about a quarter of its total energy density. Its presence is inferred from the gravitational effects that it exerts on visible matter, radiation, and the large-scale structure of the cosmos.

What is dark energy?

Dark energy is a hypothetical form of energy that is thought to account for approximately 68% of the total energy density of the Universe. It is believed to be responsible for the observed acceleration of the cosmic expansion. Dark energy may be a cosmological constant, a dynamic field, or some other exotic form of energy.

What is antimatter?

Antimatter is a material composed of antiparticles, which have the same mass as particles of ordinary matter but have opposite charge and other particle properties, such as lepton and baryon number. When a particle and its corresponding antiparticle come into contact, they annihilate each other, converting their mass into energy.
